# Managerial Accounting Map of Content (MOC)

## 1. Core Concept Hierarchy
Managerial accounting is an internal reporting system governed by **cost-benefit
analysis** rather than the rigid external rules (GAAP/IFRS) of financial 
accounting [3, 4].

*   **Foundation: Internal Reporting & Financial Analysis**
    *   **Statement of Cash Flows (Ch 13):** Reconciling net income to cash 
through Operating, Investing, and Financing activities [5, 6].
    *   **Financial Statement Analysis (Ch 14):** Normalizing data through 
**Horizontal (Trend)** and **Vertical (Common-Size)** analysis, and the 
**PALMS** ratio framework [7-9].
*   **Cost Accounting & Product Costing Systems**
    *   **Manufacturing Cost Flows (Ch 15):** Tracking **Product Costs** (DM, 
DL, MOH) through three inventory stages: **Raw Materials (RM)**, **Work in 
Process (WIP)**, and **Finished Goods (FG)** [10-12].
    *   **Job Order Costing (Ch 16):** Tracking costs for unique, customized 
products using job cost cards and **Predetermined Overhead Rates (POHR)** [13, 
14].
    *   **Process Costing (Ch 17):** Averaging costs for mass-produced 
homogeneous goods using **Equivalent Units of Production (EUP)** [15, 16].
    *   **Activity-Based Costing (Ch 18):** Refining overhead allocation using 
multiple activity pools and specific cost drivers [17, 18].
*   **Planning, Control, and Performance Evaluation**
    *   **Cost Behavior (Ch 19):** Categorizing costs as Fixed, Variable, or 
Mixed; using **High-Low** and **Regression** methods for estimation [19-21].
    *   **CVP Analysis (Ch 20):** Determining **Breakeven** points and **Target 
Profits** based on contribution margins [22-24].
    *   **Variable vs. Absorption Costing (Ch 21):** Analyzing how the treatment
of fixed overhead impacts net income and inventory valuation [25, 26].
    *   **Master Budgeting (Ch 22):** Sequential planning starting with the 
**Sales Budget** and concluding with the **Cash Budget** [27-30].
    *   **Standard Costing (Ch 23):** Performance measurement through **Variance
Analysis** (Price/Quantity and Rate/Efficiency) [31, 32].
*   **Strategic Decision Making**
    *   **Capital Budgeting (Ch 24):** Long-term investment analysis using 
**NPV**, **IRR**, **Payback Period**, and **Profitability Index** [33-35].
    *   **Relevant Costing (Ch 25):** Making short-term decisions (Special 
Orders, Make or Buy, Drop a Line) by isolating incremental/avoidable costs 
[36-38].

## 2. Formula Reference Sheet

### Financial & Ratio Analysis
*   **Horizontal (Trend) %:** $\left( \frac{\text{Analysis Year 
Amount}}{\text{Base Year Amount}} \right) \times 100\%$ [7]
*   **Current Ratio:** $\frac{\text{Current Assets}}{\text{Current 
Liabilities}}$ [39]
*   **DuPont ROE:** $\underbrace{\left( \frac{\text{Net Income}}{\text{Sales}} 
\right)}_{\text{Net Profit Margin}} \times \underbrace{\left( 
\frac{\text{Sales}}{\text{Assets}} \right)}_{\text{Asset Turnover}} \times 
\underbrace{\left( \frac{\text{Assets}}{\text{Equity}} \right)}_{\text{Equity 
Multiplier}}$ [40, 41]

### Product Costing & Behavior
*   **Cost of Goods Manufactured (COGM):** $BWIP + (DM_{used} + DL + 
MOH_{applied}) - EWIP$ [42, 43]
*   **Predetermined Overhead Rate (POHR):** $\frac{\text{Estimated Total MOH 
Cost}}{\text{Estimated Total Allocation Base}}$ [14]
*   **Equivalent Units (Weighted-Avg):** $Units Completed + (Ending WIP \times 
\% Completion)$ [44]
*   **High-Low Variable Rate ($b$):** $\frac{\text{High Cost} - \text{Low 
Cost}}{\text{High Activity} - \text{Low Activity}}$ [45]

### CVP & Decision Making
*   **Contribution Margin (CM) per Unit:** $Sales Price - Variable Cost$ [46]
*   **Breakeven in Units:** $\frac{\text{Total Fixed Costs}}{CM_{unit}}$ [23]
*   **Target Profit in Dollars:** $\frac{\text{Fixed Costs} + \text{Target 
Profit}}{CM\%}$ [24]
*   **Net Present Value (NPV):** $\sum_{t=1}^{n} \frac{CF_t}{(1+r)^t} - I_0$ 
[34]

### Variance Analysis
*   **Price/Rate Variance:** $AQ \times (AP - SP)$ [47, 48]
*   **Quantity/Efficiency Variance:** $SP \times (AQ - SQ)$ [48, 49]

## 4. Key Takeaways Summary
1.  **Forward Focus:** Managerial accounting emphasizes the future 
(budgets/projections) over historical accuracy [4].
2.  **Cost Behavior is King:** Decisions are rarely made on total unit costs; 
they rely on deconstructing costs into fixed and variable components [37, 59].
3.  **Contribution Margin vs. Gross Profit:** For internal decisions, the 
**Contribution Margin** (Sales - VC) is a more accurate measure of profitability
than Gross Profit (Sales - COGS) because it reflects cost behavior [46, 60].
4.  **Cash is the Ultimate Reality:** While accrual net income is useful for 
reporting, **Cash Flow (Ch 13)** and **Cash Budgeting (Ch 22)** are essential 
for survival and capital investment [30, 58, 61].
5.  **Allocations are Arbitrary:** Fixed cost allocations (e.g., 1/3 per 
division) can be misleading. Managers must identify **avoidable costs** to make 
correct "Make or Buy" or "Drop Product" decisions [51, 60, 62].
