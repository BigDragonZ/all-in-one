# Managerial Accounting Course Syllabus

## Course Description
This course, also known as Principles of Accounting II, focuses on the internal 
use of accounting data by managers for decision-making, planning, and control 
[1, 2]. Unlike financial accounting, which is rule-based (GAAP/IFRS) and 
historical, managerial accounting is forward-looking and relies on cost-benefit 
analysis for internal reporting [2, 3].

---

## Chapter 13: The Statement of Cash Flows
**Key Topics:**
*   Classification of activities: Operating, Investing, and Financing [4, 5].
*   Non-cash investing and financing activities (footnote disclosure) [6].
*   Indirect method vs. Direct method for operating activities [7, 8].
*   Calculating cash flows from investing (non-current assets) and financing 
(debt/equity) [9, 10].

**Learning Objectives:**
1.  Identify and classify transactions into the three categories of cash flow 
activities [11].
2.  Prepare the operating activities section by adjusting net income for 
non-cash items (depreciation/amortization) and changes in working capital 
[12-14].
3.  Reconcile beginning and ending cash balances through a complete statement 
[15, 16].

---

## Chapter 14: Financial Statement Analysis
**Key Topics:**
*   Trend (Horizontal) analysis to quantify changes over time [17, 18].
*   Common Size (Vertical) analysis for Income Statements and Balance Sheets 
[19-21].
*   The **PALMS** Ratio Framework: Profitability, Activity, Liquidity, Market, 
and Solvency [22].
*   DuPont Analysis for deconstructing Return on Equity (ROE) [23, 24].

**Learning Objectives:**
1.  Normalize financial data to compare companies of different sizes [25].
2.  Calculate and interpret liquidity (Current/Quick), activity (Turnover), and 
solvency (Debt-to-Equity) ratios [26-29].
3.  Analyze the three drivers of ROE: profitability, asset efficiency, and 
financial leverage [30, 31].

---

## Chapter 15: Introduction to Managerial Accounting & Manufacturing Cost Flows
**Key Topics:**
*   Differences between Financial and Managerial accounting [2].
*   Product costs (Direct Materials, Direct Labor, Manufacturing Overhead) vs. 
Period costs (SG&A) [32, 33].
*   Three inventory stages: Raw Materials, Work in Process (WIP), and Finished 
Goods (FG) [34, 35].
*   The Schedule of Cost of Goods Manufactured (COGM) [36, 37].

**Learning Objectives:**
1.  Classify factory-related costs as direct or indirect [38, 39].
2.  Trace the flow of costs from initial material purchase to the final Cost of 
Goods Sold (COGS) on the income statement [40-42].

---

## Chapter 16: Job Order Costing
**Key Topics:**
*   Job cost cards for tracking unique or customized products [43, 44].
*   Predetermined Manufacturing Overhead (MOH) rates [45, 46].
*   Accounting for actual vs. applied overhead [47].
*   Underapplied and overapplied overhead analysis [47, 48].

**Learning Objectives:**
1.  Calculate total job costs by summing materials, labor, and applied overhead 
[49, 50].
2.  Determine the financial impact of overhead variances at the end of a period 
[48].

---

## Chapter 17: Process Costing
**Key Topics:**
*   Costing for mass-produced, identical units (e.g., soap, petroleum) [51, 52].
*   Concept of **Equivalent Units of Production (EUP)** [53, 54].
*   The Weighted-Average method of cost allocation [55, 56].
*   Conversion costs (Direct Labor + Manufacturing Overhead) [53, 54, 56].

**Learning Objectives:**
1.  Convert physical units into equivalent units to measure work done during a 
period [57, 58].
2.  Prepare a Production Summary Report to allocate costs between finished goods
and ending WIP [59, 60].

---

## Chapter 18: Activity-Based Costing (ABC)
**Key Topics:**
*   Identifying cost pools and specific activity drivers (e.g., setups, 
inspections) [61].
*   Calculating activity rates vs. traditional plantwide rates [62, 63].
*   Assigning overhead to products based on actual activity consumption [64, 
65].

**Learning Objectives:**
1.  Apply multiple cost drivers to achieve more accurate product costing [62, 
65].
2.  Calculate the manufacturing overhead cost per unit using ABC [65].

---

## Chapter 19: Cost Behavior & Estimation
**Key Topics:**
*   Behavior of Fixed, Variable, and Mixed costs [66, 67].
*   **High-Low Method** for splitting mixed costs [68, 69].
*   Linear Regression for cost estimation [70, 71].
*   Correlation (R) and the Coefficient of Determination (R-squared) [72].

**Learning Objectives:**
1.  Develop a cost formula (Y = a + bX) to predict future total costs [71, 73].
2.  Evaluate the strength of a cost model using statistical measures [72].

---

## Chapter 20: Cost-Volume-Profit (CVP) Analysis
**Key Topics:**
*   Contribution Margin (Total and Per Unit) [74, 75].
*   Breakeven analysis in units and dollars [76, 77].
*   Target profit and target profit margin calculations [78, 79].
*   Margin of Safety and Sales Mix for multi-product companies [80-82].

**Learning Objectives:**
1.  Determine the sales volume required to reach zero profit or a specific 
profit goal [76, 78].
2.  Perform sensitivity analysis on changes in sales mix and its effect on the 
breakeven point [83].

---

## Chapter 21: Variable Costing vs. Absorption Costing
**Key Topics:**
*   Treatment of Fixed Manufacturing Overhead [84, 85].
*   Variable costing income statements (Contribution Margin format) [86, 87].
*   Absorption costing income statements (Traditional/Gross Profit format) [88, 
89].

**Learning Objectives:**
1.  Understand why net income differs between methods when production does not 
equal sales [90].
2.  Calculate the value of ending inventory under both costing methods [91, 92].

---

## Chapter 22: Master Budgeting
**Key Topics:**
*   The Sales Budget (the foundation of the Master Budget) [93, 94].
*   The Production Budget and inventory requirements [95, 96].
*   The Cash Budget: managing receipts, payments, and minimum balances [97, 98].

**Learning Objectives:**
1.  Calculate units to be produced based on sales forecasts and desired ending 
inventory [95, 99].
2.  Plan for short-term financing needs by identifying cash deficiencies and 
repayments [98, 100].

---

## Chapter 23: Standard Costing & Variance Analysis
**Key Topics:**
*   Creating a Standard Cost Card [101, 102].
*   Materials Price and Quantity variances [103, 104].
*   Labor Rate and Efficiency variances [105, 106].
*   Variable Overhead Rate and Efficiency variances [107, 108].

**Learning Objectives:**
1.  Identify "Favorable" and "Unfavorable" differences between standard and 
actual costs [109, 110].
2.  Deconstruct total variances into price/rate and quantity/efficiency 
components [104, 106].

---

## Chapter 24: Capital Budgeting
**Key Topics:**
*   The Payback Period method [111].
*   Net Present Value (NPV) and Internal Rate of Return (IRR) [112, 113].
*   Profitability Index (PI) for comparing projects of different sizes [114].

**Learning Objectives:**
1.  Evaluate long-term investment projects using the time value of money [112, 
115].
2.  Apply decision rules (Accept/Reject) based on required rates of return [116,
117].

---

## Chapter 25: Relevant Costing for Decision Making
**Key Topics:**
*   Incremental/Differential analysis [118, 119].
*   Special Order decisions (Accept or Reject?) [119, 120].
*   Make or Buy (Outsourcing) decisions and avoidable fixed costs [121-123].
*   Dropping a product line or division based on contribution margin [124, 125].

**Learning Objectives:**
1.  Distinguish between relevant costs and sunk/unavoidable costs [119, 122, 
126].
2.  Make business decisions that maximize the company's total contribution 
margin [125, 127].
