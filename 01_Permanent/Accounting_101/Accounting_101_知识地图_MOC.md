This Map of Content (MOC) provides a structured overview of the Accounting 101 
curriculum, emphasizing a pragmatic approach designed for corporate finance and 
valuation rather than accounting purity [1].

### 1. Core Concept Hierarchy
The course is structured around moving from raw accounting data to refined 
financial analysis [1, 2].

*   **I. The External Perspective:** Accounting as a historical record to 
verify, record, and report transactions [3].
*   **II. The Three Pillars (Primary Statements):** [4]
    *   **Income Statement:** Measuring performance over a period (How much did 
you earn?) [5, 6].
    *   **Balance Sheet:** A snapshot of financial position (What do you 
own/owe?) [7, 8].
    *   **Statement of Cash Flows:** Reconciling cash movements (Where did the 
cash go?) [9, 10].
*   **III. The "Cleanup" (Accounting Inconsistencies):** Adjusting historical 
data to reflect economic reality [11, 12].
    *   **Taxation:** Marginal vs. Effective vs. Cash rates [13].
    *   **Commitments:** Converting leases and content obligations into debt 
[14, 15].
    *   **Investments:** Capitalizing R&D and intangible expenses [16, 17].
*   **IV. The Standardization (Financial Ratios):** Normalizing data for 
cross-company and time-series comparison [18, 19].

### 2. Formula Reference Sheet
The following formulas represent the mathematical foundations used to 
standardize and adjust accounting data for valuation.

**The Fundamental Identity:**
$$Assets = Liabilities + Equity$$ [7]

**Operating Performance:**
$$Gross\ Profit = Revenues - COGS$$ [20]
$$Operating\ Income\ (EBIT) = Gross\ Profit - Operating\ Expenses - 
Depreciation$$ [20]
$$Net\ Income = Taxable\ Income \times (1 - t_{effective})$$ [20]

**Cash Flow Metrics:**
$$CFO = Net\ Income + Non\text{-}cash\ Expenses - \Delta Non\text{-}cash\ 
Working\ Capital$$ [21]
$$FCFE = CFO - CapEx_{Net} + (New\ Debt - Debt\ Repaid)$$ [22]

**Valuation Adjustments:**
$$PV\ of\ Leases = \sum_{t=1}^{n} \frac{Lease\ Commitment_t}{(1 + k_{debt})^t}$$
[23]
$$Adjusted\ EBIT = EBIT + Current\ Lease\ Expense - Depreciation_{Lease\ 
Asset}$$ [24]

**Standardized Ratios:**
$$ROE = \frac{Net\ Income}{Shareholders'\ Equity}$$ [25]
$$ROIC = \frac{Operating\ Income \times (1 - t)}{Invested\ Capital}$$ [25]
$$Sales\ to\ Invested\ Capital = \frac{Sales}{Total\ Debt + Equity - Cash}$$ 
[25, 26]

### 3. Cross-Chapter Concept Map
Understanding the interconnectedness of these statements is vital for a 
comprehensive financial view [27].

| Concept | Connection Point | Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Net Income** | IS $\rightarrow$ BS | Flows into Retained Earnings, 
increasing Book Equity [28, 29]. |
| **Depreciation** | IS $\rightarrow$ CF | Subtracted in IS as an expense; added
back in CFO as non-cash [21, 30]. |
| **Working Capital** | BS $\rightarrow$ CF | Changes in Current 
Assets/Liabilities on the BS drive cash inflows/outflows in CFO [31, 32]. |
| **R&D Capitalization** | IS $\rightarrow$ BS | Moves R&D from an Operating 
Expense (IS) to a Capital Asset (BS), increasing Equity and EBIT [33, 34]. |
| **Lease Accounting** | IS $\rightarrow$ BS | Converts Lease Expense (IS) into 
Debt (BS) and Interest/Depreciation (IS) [24]. |
| **Stock-Based Comp** | IS $\rightarrow$ CF | Reported as an expense (IS); 
often incorrectly added back to cash flow as "non-cash" [14, 35]. |

### 4. Key Takeaways Summary
*   **Accounting is History:** Accountants are historians, not appraisers or 
forecasters; their data is a starting point, not the final value [3, 36].
*   **The Life Cycle Context:** A company’s stage (Young, Growth, Mature, 
Declining) dictates the "normal" patterns of its margins, cash flows, and debt 
levels [37-40].
*   **Accrual is Not Cash:** Accrual accounting matches revenues and expenses to
the transaction time, necessitating the Statement of Cash Flows to find the 
"honest" cash truth [41, 42].
*   **Intangibles are Assets:** Modern accounting fails to capture R&D and 
brands; analysts must capitalize these to understand true profitability and 
invested capital [16, 34, 43].
*   **Standardization is Mandatory:** Absolute numbers are "apples and oranges";
ratios like ROIC and Sales to Invested Capital are required to compare firms 
fairly [18, 44].
*   **Less is More:** Avoid the "noise" of 100+ ratios; pick the few that build 
a future-oriented story for the specific business [45].
