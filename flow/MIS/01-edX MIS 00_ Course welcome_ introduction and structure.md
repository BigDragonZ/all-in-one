## 精修内容

Hello, welcome to this introduction to Management Information Systems, MIS: A Survival Guide. My name is Alfonso Duran. You may know me from the presentation video. I am a professor of Engineering Management at UC3M and coordinator of this MOOC, and I will be the narrator in its videos. We shall be seeing each other quite a bit in the forthcoming weeks. Even though you won't see them in the videos, you are also likely to interact and receive support from my colleagues. Our profiles are in the course's presentation page, and our course mascot, Missy, is also eager to help. I hope you will find this course a helpful and productive investment of your time.

Management Information Systems permeate our professional and personal life. We all use them to carry out our banking operations, buy things online, and for countless purposes at our workplace. Sometimes, however, they can be a little confusing, and quite often, they can be quite annoying in their stubborn insistence that you do things precisely their way.

This course will provide an overall introduction to what Management Information Systems are and how they shape day-to-day business life and our current and future jobs. We will cover the support infrastructures such as the cloud or Big Data, and the main integrated systems—the ERPs, such as SAP or open-source alternatives like Odoo. This introductory course will generally adopt a light touch and a managerial, rather than purely technological, focus. We will use analogies to illustrate key concepts, resort to frequent examples to illuminate explanations, and strive to strike a proper balance between clarity, amenity, and conceptual soundness.

May I suggest that you have a look at the syllabus? You will find useful information there regarding the content of the course, the detailed structure, and the evaluation procedures. This edition of the course is self-paced, so all required materials will be available from the first day, and you can progress through its content according to your own schedule. To provide some tentative guidance, however, we have kept the original weekly structure that we used in the first edition. You can simply think of the six weeks as six units, or you can use this duration as a tentative time frame to motivate yourself to work continuously and fend off the temptation to procrastinate and leave everything for tomorrow, for *mañana*.

The course is structured as follows:

1.  **Week 1:** We will analyze what Management Information Systems are and how they fit within the overall Information and Communication Technologies (ICTs) environment. We will also discuss how companies obtain their MIS, either by purchasing ready-made packages, through bespoke development, or by using open systems.
2.  **Week 2:** This week will be devoted to networks and distributed information systems, particularly client-server models.
3.  **Week 3:** We shall examine our raw material—data—and discuss databases and the very fashionable area of Big Data.
4.  **Week 4:** We will address the "top predators" of MIS: the integrated information systems known as ERPs (Enterprise Resource Planning systems) and their associated business processes. You will carry out a hands-on assignment in which you will access a real ERP to perform a set of real-life transactions.
5.  **Week 5:** This week will be dedicated to the challenges involved in implementing and customizing ERPs.
6.  **Week 6:** To finalize the course, we shall address the role played by Management Information Systems in information-driven decision-making and conclude with some reflections about the future.

You may be wondering if you need a previous background in MIS to follow the course. There is no reason to worry. This is an introductory course, so no previous knowledge is taken for granted. Nevertheless, I honestly believe that even those already familiar with ICTs and Management Information Systems will also benefit from this course. I look forward to a challenging, rewarding, and hopefully fun course. I will see you in the videos of the first unit. Goodbye for now.
