### 什么是预算？

What is a budget? Simply put, a budget is a financial statement. From an organization's point of view, preparing a budget gives us clarity about what we are doing, how the organization is performing, how we should run it, and how we need to plan for the future. Everything depends on this financial statement, which is the budget.

In one sentence, you can say: **A budget is a monetary or quantitative expression of business plans and policies to be pursued in a future period of time.** A budget is always for the future. For example, my budget for the next six months is X. Budgets can be prepared for one year, three months, six months, or even one month. Unlike a country's budget which is typically annual, an organization's budget period can vary depending on its activities and size. It can be for one month, three months, or any other period. The key is that you are preparing a statement for the future.

### 预算的特点 (Characteristics of a Budget)

Let's look at the characteristics of a budget.

- **基于历史数据 (Based on past figures):** How do we plan a budget? It is mainly based on past figures. For instance, I might observe the budgets of the last five years and plan the future budget accordingly. You can't prepare a budget blindly without any past data or information. To write a budget, we must know the history and the past.

- **灵活性 (Flexible):** Whenever you are preparing a budget, it must be flexible. Things are always changing, whether it's technology, the economy, or anything else. We must be ready to adapt to these changes. A rigid budget will not work because it lacks flexibility. When a budget is flexible, we can change it if something unexpected happens, making it more effective.

- **多层级参与 (Various levels involved in preparation):** All levels of management—higher, middle, and lower—must be involved while preparing the budget. Why? Because the entire organization's activities are involved. The lower-level management often has more practical knowledge because they are more actively involved in day-to-day operations. The higher and middle levels get information from the lower level. Therefore, involving all levels ensures the budget is more comprehensive and perfect.

- **具体明确的陈述 (Specific statement):** Whatever budget you are preparing, it should be specific. For example, "Budget for one month for X company," or "Sales budget for Y branch." You need to specify what kind of budget it is—a cash budget, a flexible budget, or a sales budget. Without specific details, the budget lacks clarity.

- **高层管理参与 (Top management involved):** While all levels should be involved, the top level *must* be involved. Why? Because the ultimate decision-making authority lies with the top-level management. Their involvement is mandatory to ensure the budget aligns with the overall strategy and they have a good understanding of the plan.

### 编制预算的必要性 (Need for Preparing a Budget)

Why do we need to prepare a budget?

- **制定未来政策 (Formulating future policies):** A budget is essential for formulating future policies. Without a budget, we have no idea what's going on, what we are spending, or what we are planning. It provides the foundation for future actions.

- **确保各部门间的协调 (To ensure coordination among various departments):** Budget preparation involves all types of management and departments, such as sales, purchasing, finance, production, and HR. When all department heads are involved and have knowledge about the budget, they will coordinate and move together towards a common goal.

- **作为组织控制的工具 (To act as a tool to control the organization):** Once a budget is prepared, everyone is expected to follow it. They keep the budget in mind and act accordingly. This creates a sense of control throughout the organization, from the lowest-level employee to the top management.

- **激励员工 (To motivate various employees):** When a budget is in place, every employee knows the targets. This knowledge motivates them to work towards achieving the budget goals. They know the deadlines and the cost limits. This clarity motivates employees to perform, implement plans according to the budget, and strive to achieve the set targets. If there's no budget, people might adopt a "let it go" attitude.

### 预算的优势 (Advantages of Budgeting)

- **提高效率 (Improves efficiency):** An organization's efficiency improves with a budget because it sets targets, allows for performance tracking against those targets, and facilitates evaluation. These three elements—targets, performance, and evaluation—drive efficiency.

- **促进协调 (Coordination):** A budget ensures good coordination among all levels of employees—top, middle, and lower management. Everyone moves together towards the common goal defined by the budget.

- **实现经济效益 (Economy):** A budget helps in achieving economy, or cost savings. If a target is set, for example, a budget of 1 crore for the coming year, everyone will work to stay below that limit. They might even complete the work for 80 lakhs, saving 20 lakhs. Without a target, spending might exceed 1 crore.

- **增强员工意识 (Consciousness among employees):** Budgeting makes employees more conscious and alert. They feel they are a part of the organization because they are involved in the budget preparation, organizational activities, and decision-making. This sense of involvement and knowledge leads to better results.

- **时间限制 (Time-bound):** A budget is always time-bound. We prepare it for a specific period, like one month or two months. This creates a target for that period, and after its completion, we move to the next target and the next budget. Budgeting is a continuous process as long as the organization is operational, whether it's monthly, quarterly, or yearly.
