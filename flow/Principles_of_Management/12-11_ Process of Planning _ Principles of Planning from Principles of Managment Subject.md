# 计划的流程与原则

## 计划的流程

Hello, dear students. Welcome to Devika's Commerce and Management Academy. Today, we'll discuss the process of planning and the principles of planning. This is a very important topic for commerce and management students. Regardless of your course, you cannot escape this topic because understanding the planning process and its principles is crucial. If you don't know them, your efforts may be useless.

You've gained some understanding of what planning is from previous videos. Now, let's see the process. How do we proceed? Planning is for the future, and the future is uncertain. We are making a plan for an uncertain future, which may or may not be executed perfectly. However, it must be implemented properly to a relevant extent. If you know the procedure, you'll understand the precautions we need to take and what to remember while planning.

There are a total of eight steps in the planning process. You have to follow them step-by-step.

1.  **识别行动需求 (Recognizing the Need for Action)**
    First of all, you have to identify the problem. You anticipate a problem that may occur in the future and realize the need to make a plan for it. This starts with identifying the problem. Once you identify it, you focus on the goals. How do you take action? Based on the goals. You'll be able to take action by considering the pros and cons of a particular problem or aspect. Identify the problem and its most important aspects.

2.  **收集必要信息 (Gathering Necessary Information)**
    Once you know there is a problem, you must gather information from every angle: from an economic perspective, from the product's point of view, from the customer's point of view, and many others. Gather all the information and think about the pros and cons of that particular problem. When you have all the information, you can move to the next step.

3.  **制定目标 (Laying Down Objectives)**
    What are the objectives? Objectives are directly related to the goals. If you know your ambition or your goals, you can then focus on the objectives. After identifying the problem and gathering information, you identify the objectives of the planning. To do this, you must consider many factors, both internal and external to the organization. Only then can you frame the objectives of the plan.

4.  **确定计划前提 (Determining Planning Premises)**
    This step is related to forecasting. Planning is for an uncertain future, but you identify the planning process based on forecasting. We have to consider a few important aspects:
    *   **产品需求 (Demand of the product):** We cannot blindly create a plan. We must know the demand for the product.
    *   **数量和成本 (Volume and Cost):** How much quantity is required (in tons, quintals, etc.)? The cost of the product also makes a lot of difference, especially in developing countries where a product with good quality at a cheaper rate will become popular.
    *   **原材料和劳动力供应 (Supply of raw material and labor):** For every product, we need raw materials. Is it available? We also need to consider the availability of labor. If both are available, 50% of the problem is solved.
    *   **消费者偏好 (Consumer preferences):** What are the tastes and preferences of the consumers? This changes with technology and fashion.
    *   **政府政策 (Government policies):** Government policies are always changing, and we have to adapt accordingly.
    All these things are considered when determining the planning premises.

5.  **审查备选行动方案 (Examining Alternative Courses of Action)**
    If Plan A isn't going to work, you need a Plan B. If Plan B also doesn't work in a given situation, you need a Plan C. You should have several alternative courses of action. Go for different plans. Keep at least four or five plans ready because trends are always changing and nothing is fixed.

6.  **评估行动模式 (Evaluating Action Patterns)**
    We have considered alternative courses of action like A, B, and C. If we decide Plan A is going to work, how do we proceed with it? This is what we call the evaluation of the action pattern. For example, if I have a project XYZ and I've gone through all the previous steps and decided it's a good project, how do I proceed? You have to go in a systematic way, step one, step two, and so on.

7.  **确定次要计划 (Determining Secondary Plans)**
    Secondary plans are supportive plans. For the main plan (e.g., Plan A) to be implemented, what supportive actions are needed? We need to have different supportive action plans ready, such as point one, point two, point three. These secondary action plans are directly interrelated with the main action plan and make its implementation easier.

8.  **实施计划 (Implementing the Plans)**
    After all the previous steps, the final step is to implement the plan. We have to be very careful during implementation. We have already prepared alternatives. If we decide to implement Plan A but find it's not working, we can just change to Plan B or Plan C according to the situation, the need, and the requirement.

These are the eight steps of the planning process.

## 计划的原则

If you understand the planning process, the principles of planning will be easy. You can almost write these points blindly. There are also eight principles.

1.  **目标贡献原则 (Principle of Contribution to Objectives)**
    This principle states that any plan should be supportive of and contribute to the goals and objectives of the organization. The planning you prepare must align with the organization's goals.

2.  **首要性原则 (Principle of Primacy of Planning)**
    This refers to the main objective of the organization. The plan must support and contribute to this primary objective. Is the plan supportive? Is it going to be implemented? This is the principle of the primacy of planning.

3.  **计划前提原则 (Principle of Planning Premises)**
    As discussed in the process, we must consider all the premises related to forecasting for an uncertain future. This includes factors like demand, cost, resources, and government policies.

4.  **备选方案原则 (Principle of Alternatives)**
    This is the same point as in the process. Always think about different alternatives (A, B, C). You can decide which one to use based on the situation.

5.  **时机原则 (Principle of Timing)**
    When should you implement the plan? You have to implement it at the right time. If you delay, there's no use. The timing depends on the situation, requirements, market conditions, and economic conditions.

6.  **灵活性原则 (Principle of Flexibility)**
    Once a plan is prepared, it shouldn't be rigid. It should always be flexible. If one approach isn't working, let's try another. Let's make some changes. The plan must be adaptable.

7.  **承诺原则 (Principle of Commitment)**
    Who prepares the plan? Experts in their field, a team of people. Once a plan is prepared, it should be implemented properly with commitment. The team must be committed to preparing and implementing it until it is successfully executed. Their commitment, work, and dedication make a lot of difference.

8.  **竞争策略原则 (Principle of Competitive Strategies)**
    You must consider the plans, companies, and policies of your competitors. Your strategy must be competitive. If other companies are doing something, you might need to do something different or make changes to attract customers to your company.

These are the eight principles of planning. You don't need to write them exactly as stated if you understand the core concepts from the planning steps, objectives, and this introduction. For each point, two to three lines of explanation are enough.
