# What is Logistics - Reverse Logistics and Third Party Logistics

Logistics is defined as the process of planning, implementing, and controlling the efficient, effective flow and storage of goods, services, and related information from their point of origin to the point of consumption for the purpose of satisfying customer requirements. This pertains to either **inbound logistics**, where we receive material from our supplier into our organization, or **outbound logistics**, in which we ship out material, inventory, or products to our customers. In essence, logistics is the movement of supplies and materials through the supply chain. Every time something is put on a truck, a ship, a boat, or a container, it is considered logistics.

We also have what we call **Global Logistics**, which is the exact same thing but from a global perspective, not a regional one. This is when we have containers put on ships, often coming from China, because many U.S. companies produce products there. Global Logistics and logistics are basically the same thing; Global Logistics is just on a global scale.

Another type is called **Reverse Logistics**. This is simply when we use logistics in a way that reverses the process. This usually applies to recycling material or returning a damaged product. A warranty claim is another example. If you have a refrigerator with a 10-year warranty and it breaks after three years, the manufacturer would come, take it, and either fix or replace it. That is a form of reverse logistics.

Another form is what we call **Third-Party Logistics** or **3PL**. This is when a company uses a third party to outsource its distribution, warehousing, and fulfillment services. In a general sense, if you go to the post office to send a shipment to a friend, you're using a third party. You're outsourcing your logistics or transportation. In that case, you're using 3PL. Anytime you pay someone or outsource your transportation, you're using third-party logistics.

Key takeaways:
*   **Logistics** is the movement of supplies and material through a supply chain.
*   **Global Logistics** is the same thing, but on a global scale.
*   **Third-Party Logistics (3PL)** is when a company uses a third party to outsource its distribution, warehousing, and fulfillment.
*   **Reverse Logistics** is the reverse process, used for recycling, reusing material, warranty claims, or returning damaged goods.
