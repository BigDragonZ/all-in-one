First, let's calculate **Accounts Receivable**. We know the **receivables turnover** is 8. The formula for receivables turnover is **Net Sales / Accounts Receivable**. We know from the income statement that Net Sales is $100,000. Since our sales are eight times larger than our accounts receivable, we can calculate accounts receivable by taking $100,000 divided by 8, which gives us $12,500.

Next, we'll calculate **Inventory**. We know the **inventory turnover** is 4. The formula is **Cost of Goods Sold / Inventory**. The income statement shows our Cost of Goods Sold is $66,000. To find the inventory value, we take $66,000 divided by 4, which equals $16,500. Now we can calculate **Total Current Assets** by summing Cash ($10,000), Accounts Receivable ($12,500), and Inventory ($16,500), which totals $39,000.

Now, let's determine **Total Assets**. We'll use the **Return on Assets (ROA)**, which is 7.5%. The formula is **Net Income / Total Assets**. We know Net Income is $9,000. To find Total Assets, we divide $9,000 by 7.5% (0.075), which means our Total Assets must be $120,000. According to the accounting equation (Assets = Liabilities + Equity), our **Total Liabilities and Equity** must also be $120,000. With this, we can figure out our **Plant Assets**. By subtracting Total Current Assets ($39,000) from Total Assets ($120,000), we find that our Plant Assets are $81,000.

Let's move to the liabilities and equity section. We'll use the **debt ratio**, which is 35%. The formula is **Total Debt / Total Assets**. We know Total Assets are $120,000. Therefore, our **Total Debt** (which is the same as Total Liabilities) is $120,000 times 35%, which equals $42,000. Since we were given Accounts Payable (a current liability) of $14,000, we can calculate our **Long-term Liabilities** by subtracting $14,000 from $42,000, which gives us $28,000.

Finally, to find **Stockholders' Equity**, we subtract Total Liabilities ($42,000) from Total Liabilities and Equity ($120,000). This means Stockholders' Equity must be $78,000, and our balance sheet is now complete.
