### 初始问题与数据

Let's get started with our problem. We have Zulu Company, and we only know three things:
1.  The sales price is $80 per unit.
2.  The variable cost is 65% of sales.
3.  The total fixed cost is $42,000.

From this, we can derive more information. We'll use a variable costing income statement format with columns for total dollars, per-unit dollars, and percentages. We know that sales are always 100%. Since variable costs are 65%, our contribution margin percentage is 35% (100% - 65%). The contribution margin per unit is $28 ($80 * 35%), and the variable cost per unit is $52 ($80 * 65%).

### 1. 计算盈亏平衡点

Let's calculate the breakeven point, which is the point of zero profit where total revenues equal total expenses.

To calculate the **breakeven point in units**, we divide the total fixed cost by the contribution margin per unit:
$42,000 / $28 = **1,500 units**.

To find the **breakeven point in dollars**, we divide the total fixed cost by the contribution margin percentage:
$42,000 / 35% = **$120,000**.

We can verify this: 1,500 units * $80/unit = $120,000.

Let's prove this is the point of zero profit by building the income statement for 1,500 units:
- **Sales**: 1,500 units * $80 = $120,000
- **Variable Costs**: $120,000 * 65% = $78,000
- **Contribution Margin**: $120,000 - $78,000 = $42,000
- **Fixed Costs**: $42,000
- **Net Income (Profit)**: $42,000 - $42,000 = **$0**

If we sell less than 1,500 units, we lose money. If we sell more, we make a profit.

### 2. 计算目标利润

Now, let's calculate the sales needed for a **target profit of $7,000**. The formula is (Fixed Costs + Target Profit) / Contribution Margin.

- **In Units**: ($42,000 + $7,000) / $28 per unit = $49,000 / $28 = **1,750 units**.
- **In Dollars**: ($42,000 + $7,000) / 35% = $49,000 / 0.35 = **$140,000**.

Let's prove that selling 1,750 units results in a $7,000 profit:
- **Sales**: 1,750 units * $80 = $140,000
- **Variable Costs**: $140,000 * 65% = $91,000
- **Contribution Margin**: $140,000 - $91,000 = $49,000
- **Fixed Costs**: $42,000
- **Net Income (Profit)**: $49,000 - $42,000 = **$7,000**

### 3. 计算目标利润率

What if we want a **target profit *margin* of 10%** of sales? This is a different calculation.

We know the contribution margin is 35% of sales. If the profit is 10% of sales, then the remaining portion of the contribution margin must cover the fixed costs. Therefore, fixed costs must equal 25% of sales (35% - 10%). We can use this to find the required sales in dollars:
$42,000 (Fixed Costs) / 25% = **$168,000 (Total Sales)**.

To achieve $168,000 in sales, we need to sell **2,100 units** ($168,000 / $80 per unit). Let's verify the profit margin at this level:
- **Sales**: 2,100 units * $80 = $168,000
- **Variable Costs**: $168,000 * 65% = $109,200
- **Contribution Margin**: $168,000 - $109,200 = $58,800
- **Fixed Costs**: $42,000
- **Net Income (Profit)**: $58,800 - $42,000 = **$16,800**

The profit margin is $16,800 / $168,000, which is exactly **10%**.

### 4. 计算安全边际

Finally, let's calculate the margin of safety, assuming our current sales level is 2,100 units ($168,000). The margin of safety is the cushion between current sales and breakeven sales.

- **Margin of Safety in Units**: 2,100 (current units) - 1,500 (breakeven units) = **600 units**.
- **Margin of Safety in Dollars**: $168,000 (current sales) - $120,000 (breakeven sales) = **$48,000**.
- **Margin of Safety Percentage**: This shows how much sales can drop before we incur a loss. We can calculate it using either units or dollars: 600 units / 2,100 units = **28.57%**.

This means our sales can drop by 28.57% before we hit the breakeven point. A 5% or 10% drop in sales will not cause a loss.
