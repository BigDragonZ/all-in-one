### 利润率分析 (Profit Margins)

**1. 毛利率 (Gross Profit Margin)**

Let's start with the **Gross Profit Margin**. We take our gross profit ($170,000) and divide it by our sales. This gives us 40.42%. This means for every dollar in sales, we generate about 40 cents in gross profit, and our cost of goods sold is therefore about 60%. This is a slight improvement over last year's margin.

**2. 营业利润率 (Operating Profit Margin)**

Next is the **Operating Profit Margin**. For this, we need Earnings Before Interest and Taxes (EBIT), which is synonymous with operating profit. We take the operating profit ($61,000) and divide it by net sales. This results in 14.47%, meaning about 14.5 cents of every sales dollar is left as operating profit. It's important to present these ratios as percentages, not decimals.

**3. 净利润率 (Net Profit Margin)**

The third ratio is the **Net Profit Margin**, also known as **Return on Sales (ROS)**. We calculate this by taking the final net income ($40,000) and dividing it by sales, which gives us 9.64%. If someone refers to "profit margin" without specifying, they usually mean the net profit margin, but it's always best to clarify. Our net profit margin has significantly improved from 5.3% last year, which is a positive sign.

### 资产与权益回报率

**4. 总资产回报率 (Return on Assets - ROA)**

Now let's look at **Return on Assets (ROA)**. This ratio measures how effectively we use our assets to generate profit. We calculate it by dividing net income by total assets. Our current ROA is 11.4%, which means for every dollar invested in assets, we are generating 11.4 cents in net income. This is an improvement from the previous year, indicating better profitability and efficiency.

**5. 净资产收益率 (Return on Equity - ROE)**

Next is **Return on Equity (ROE)**, which is calculated by dividing net income by shareholders' equity. Our ROE is 23.36%. This ratio measures the return generated for the owners' investment. ROE will typically be higher than ROA as long as the company has debt, because equity is a smaller base than total assets (Assets = Liabilities + Equity). If a company had zero debt, its ROA and ROE would be identical. Our ROE has more than doubled from last year's 11.84%, which is excellent for our investors.

### 杜邦分析 (DuPont Equation)

**6. 杜邦方程 (DuPont Equation)**

The **DuPont equation** provides a way to deconstruct ROE into its core components, offering deeper insight into what drives the return. Developed at the DuPont Corporation in the 1920s, the formula breaks ROE into three parts: Profitability, Asset Efficiency, and Financial Leverage.

**ROE = (Net Profit Margin) x (Total Asset Turnover) x (Equity Multiplier)**
**ROE = (Net Income / Sales) x (Sales / Assets) x (Assets / Equity)**

Mathematically, the "Sales" and "Assets" terms cancel out, leaving the original ROE formula (Net Income / Equity).

Let's calculate each component:
1.  **Net Profit Margin (Return on Sales):** We already calculated this as 9.64%.
2.  **Total Asset Turnover:** This measures asset efficiency. It's calculated as Sales divided by Assets, which is 1.18. For every dollar in assets, we generate $1.18 in sales.
3.  **Equity Multiplier:** This measures financial leverage. It's calculated as Assets divided by Equity, which is 2.05.

Multiplying these three components (9.64% x 1.18 x 2.05) gives us 23.36%, which is exactly the ROE we calculated earlier. The DuPont analysis shows how profitability (Net Profit Margin), asset efficiency (Turnover), and debt (Equity Multiplier) combine to determine the return on equity. For instance, increasing debt lowers equity, which increases the Equity Multiplier and, in turn, boosts ROE.
