Hello and welcome. Today we're working in managerial accounting on the Cost Volume Profit analysis chapter, or CVP analysis, and we're doing the very beginning of breakeven analysis, the very first part of Cost Volume Profit.

Let's get started. We have the X-ray company, and we know just three things. We know the sales price is going to be $70 per unit, the variable cost is $42 per unit, and the fixed cost is going to be $39,200. We're going to answer a couple of things here: What is the contribution margin per unit? What's the contribution margin percentage? And we're going to calculate the breakeven in units and the breakeven in dollars.

On this type of problem, we're going to take our sales and subtract our variable cost. Sales minus variable cost equals the contribution margin (CM). This is how much it contributes to covering our fixed cost. So, this is going to be $70 minus $42, which is $28. $28 is our contribution margin.

It's helpful to also think about the percentages. We're always going to make the sales to be 100%. If I take the variable cost of $42 divided by the sales price of $70, I'm going to get 60%. Then our contribution margin is $28 divided by the $70, and that's going to be 40%. So you could say, "Well, how profitable are you per unit?" After we take our sales and our variable cost, we're making $28 on each one. Or if you want to think in terms of dollars, we're making 40 cents of every dollar. When we make a sale and receive dollars, 40% of that becomes contribution margin. So, what is our contribution margin per unit? It's $28. And what's our contribution margin percentage? That's 40%.

Now, to get breakeven in units and breakeven in dollars, we need to know the formula. The formula for breakeven in units is `Fixed Cost / Contribution Margin per Unit`. The formula for breakeven in dollars is `Fixed Cost / Contribution Margin Percentage`. Let's think about what the breakeven point is. It's the point where revenues equal expenses, or it's the point where we have zero profit.

Our total fixed cost here was given at $39,200. To find the breakeven in units, we're going to divide it by the contribution margin per unit of $28. So, how many units do we have to sell to reach the breakeven point? $39,200 divided by $28 gives us 1,400 units. We have to sell 1,400 units before we stop losing money and start making a profit. This is the point of zero profit. So, our breakeven in units is 1,400.

What is breakeven in dollars? One thing we could do is take the 1,400 units times the $70 price. But let's use the formula. Our fixed cost is $39,200, and our contribution margin percentage is 40%. So, what's the level of sales we need to reach? $39,200 divided by 40% is going to be $98,000. $98,000 is our breakeven in dollars.

Now, let's start thinking about the variable costing income statement, or as it's sometimes called, the contribution margin income statement. We're going to build this income statement three different ways: based on total dollars, per unit, and as percentages. This will be helpful when we get to later topics like target profit.

Let's build the statement at the breakeven point of 1,400 units.
- **Per Unit**: Sales price is $70, variable cost is $42, so the contribution margin is $28.
- **Percentages**: Sales is 100%, variable cost is 60%, so the contribution margin is 40%.
- **Total Dollars**:
    - Sales: 1,400 units * $70/unit = $98,000.
    - Variable Costs: We can calculate this two ways: 1,400 units * $42/unit, or 60% of total sales ($98,000 * 60%). Both give us $58,800.
    - Contribution Margin: $98,000 - $58,800 = $39,200.
    - Fixed Costs: The total fixed cost is given as $39,200.
    - Profit: $39,200 (Contribution Margin) - $39,200 (Fixed Costs) = $0.
This proves our breakeven point is correct. At this level of sales, our profit is exactly zero.

This income statement model shows what happens when we change the number of units.
- If we sold only 1,000 units, our sales would be $70,000, variable costs $42,000, and contribution margin $28,000. After subtracting the $39,200 in fixed costs, we would have a loss of $11,200.
- If we sold 1,500 units, we would have a profit of $2,800.
- If we made sales of 2,000 units, we would have a profit of $16,800, which is a profit of $8.40 per unit or a 12% profit margin.

This is how you think about the contribution margin income statement and the very basic idea of breakeven analysis.
