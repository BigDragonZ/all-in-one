Let's get started. Valco Inc. has the following budgeted sales in units for four months: January (3,500), February (4,100), March (5,300), and April (5,000). The company has a policy to maintain an ending inventory equal to 25% of the following month's sales. We are also told that the ending inventory for December was 400 units, which will be the beginning inventory for January. Our task is to prepare a production budget for January, February, and March.

The template for a production budget starts with the budgeted sales for the period. To that, we add the target ending inventory to determine the total required units. Then, we subtract the beginning inventory to find out how many units we need to produce.

Let's begin with January. The budgeted sales are 3,500 units. The target ending inventory is 25% of the following month's (February's) sales. So, we take February's sales of 4,100 units and multiply by 25%, which gives us 1,025 units. The total required units for January are the sum of budgeted sales and target ending inventory: 3,500 + 1,025 = 4,525 units. We then subtract the beginning inventory, which was 400 units. So, the required production for January is 4,525 - 400 = 4,125 units. Even though sales are only 3,500, we need to produce more to build our inventory from 400 to 1,025 units.

Now for February, we follow the same pattern. Budgeted sales are 4,100 units. The target ending inventory is 25% of March's sales, which are 5,300 units. 5,300 times 25% is 1,325 units. The total required units for February are 4,100 + 1,325 = 5,425 units. The beginning inventory for February is the ending inventory from January, which we calculated as 1,025 units. Therefore, the required production for February is 5,425 - 1,025 = 4,400 units.

Finally, let's calculate the production for March. Budgeted sales are 5,300 units. The target ending inventory is 25% of April's sales, which are 5,000 units. 5,000 times 25% equals 1,250 units. The total required units for March are 5,300 + 1,250 = 6,550 units. The beginning inventory for March is February's ending inventory, which was 1,325 units. So, the required production for March is 6,550 - 1,325 = 5,225 units.

This is our production budget for the three months. If you build this in Excel, you can create a flexible template. For example, you could change the inventory policy from 25% to 10%, and you would see that we need to produce less each month. If you changed it to 50%, we would need to produce more to maintain a larger inventory.

I hope this was helpful for you to understand how to create a production budget. This budget provides a plan for how many units to produce each month. Everything in the master budget is built upon the sales budget, followed by the production budget. From here, we can calculate budgets for materials, labor, and overhead.
